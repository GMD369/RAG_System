Chapter 05

How Text Gets Broken Down

The way you split documents dramatically affects retrieval quality. Chunks that are too big waste the LLM's context window. Chunks that are too small lose surrounding context. And how you split matters: a naive character split might cut mid-sentence, while a semantic splitter keeps related ideas together.

Four Approaches

🔪

Character Splitter

Splits on a single separator (e.g. double newline). Fast but brittle — long paragraphs without the separator won't be split at all.

Simplest
🔄

Recursive Character

Tries separators in order: \n\n → \n → . → space → "". Gracefully degrades — always produces correctly sized chunks.

Recommended
🧮

Semantic Chunker

Embeds every sentence, then splits where embedding similarity drops sharply. Topic-aware splits — no arbitrary size limit needed.

Smart
🤖

Agentic Chunker

Sends the text to an LLM and asks it to decide boundaries. Most flexible — the model understands context and narrative flow.

Expensive
